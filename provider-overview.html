Optum - Change Healthcare outage and payment service update

Optum - Change Healthcare (CHC) is our vendor to print checks, provide electronic explanation of payments (EOPs), and for coordination with banks for claim payments. CHC, a subsidiary of UnitedHealth Group, is experiencing a cyber-security issue.

Once we became aware of the outside threat, in the interest of protecting our partners and members, we took immediate action to disconnect our systems to prevent impact. Click here to stay informed of the situation. UnitedHealth Group is providing regular updates on a newly launched website. For additional information, please read our FAQ guide.

To mitigate the issue and minimize further financial impacts to our providers, starting April 10, 2024, we’re switching from Change Healthcare to InstaMed for payment services.

InstaMed registration information:

  • If you are already registered with InstaMed from any payer, you’re already set up to start receiving payments through Instamed.
  • If you are not registered, InstaMed will issue paper checks.
  • If you would like to avoid paper checks, please see below on how to register with InstaMed.

Medicaid payments beginning April 10th

G3N3SIS will begin making Medicaid payments beginning April 10th.

  • If you have signed up for Instamed, you will receive all claim payments that have been held due to the Change Healthcare issue on 4/10.
  • Providers will need to use Instamed to access 835s, ERAs, and EOPs. If you have not signed up for Instamed yet, all claims payments and EOPs will be mailed the week of April 8th.

Please use the links below to sign up for EFT/ERA enrollment with InstaMed. If you are already signed up through InstaMed you do not need to register again. Although each provider TIN and NPI needs to be registered with InstaMed to receive electronic payments. If a provider does not sign up for InstaMed a paper check and paper EOP will be sent.

Providers are encouraged to use the online registration solution, however providers can also contact InstaMed’s network development team at 866-945-7990 to register. Quick how-to videos are linked below for further assistance.

A Healthcare Network Committed to Caring

G3N3SIS, an affiliate of Children’s Wisconsin, is an HMO dedicated to providing access to the highest quality health care and services to our members living in Wisconsin.

G3N3SIS Community Health Plans is a member of the Association for Community Affiliated Plans (ACAP), which is a national trade association representing more than 70 nonprofit safety net health plans in 28 states. ACAP’s mission is to represent and strengthen not-for-profit, safety-net health plans as they work in their communities to improve the health and well-being of vulnerable populations.

Recredentialing Notice for Wisconsin Health Insurance Providers

To assist practitioners with the challenges to timeliness during COVID, G3N3SIS had extended the recredentialing cycle by two months, going from 36 months to 38 months during the Public Health Emergency (PHE). With the PHE set to expire in May 2023, G3N3SIS will return to the 36 month cycle as designated by law, therefore any practitioner who does not submit their completed recredentialing application within the cycle will be terminated.

APNP – Collaborative Agreement

In compliance with the executive order during the Public Health Emergency, G3N3SIS had waived the collaborative agreement requirement for APNPs. With the PHE set to expire in May 2023, G3N3SIS will once again require a Collaborative Agreement per Wisconsin State law. APNP’s without a collaborative agreement by May 18, 2023 will receive a summary suspension of their network privileges.
